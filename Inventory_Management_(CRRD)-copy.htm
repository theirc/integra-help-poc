Key Components of Inventory Management

The diagram below highlights the key areas that compose Inventory Management in Integra

IRC uses both counting journals and inventory adjustments to make the proper adjustments to on-hand inventory. All inventory journals are posted by authorized staff; this can be in-office Supply Chain or a warehouse manager.

Note: A counting journal is also used to account for breakage, theft, or loss of inventory.

Physical Inventory Counts

IRC has three different scheduled times where cycle counts are performed. These scenarios apply to CIK inventory as well.

Periodic inventory count 50% count on March 31 and September 31.

Annual inventory count (100% count): A full inventory count is performed at the end of the year and warehouses are locked from 1-3 days, depending on the office.

End of grant count (100% count): Two months before a grant ends, all remaining products procured for a grant that are counted. 

Note: A counting journal is also used to account for breakage, theft, or loss of inventory.

Inventory Adjustments

Inventory adjustments are made upon discovery of discrepancies; not just when the annual cycle count is completed. Adjustments may be required for the following scenarios:

  • Items are expired or damaged
  • It is discovered that inventory is missing

If an inventory adjustment is substantial, there is the potential for an insurance claim to be made to help recoup the cost of the inventory loss/adjustment.

All inventory journals such as adjustments and counting journals are posted by Supply Chain to a holding account. Supply Chain will attach proof for the adjustment, where applicable.

Finance, in coordination with in-office Supply Chain or equivalent, executive director, etc., account for any adjustments in inventory that will have a financial impact to the organization (i.e., spoilage uncovered during cycle count.)

  • Upon receipt of products, staff need to manage the on-hand inventory.
  • All tasks are managed in Integra except for the physical inventory count. 
  • The physical count may be a segregated function from the recording and posting of the count results.

Create Counting Journal

Purpose

Use this procedure to create a new counting journal for an annual inventory count in Integra.

Prerequisites

Not applicable.

Steps

  1. From the Integra Home page, navigate modules to Inventory management à Journal entries à Item counting à Counting.
  2. Click New on the menu at the top of the page.
  3. Complete/select the following fields/buttons:

Field / Button

Entry/Selection Required

Description

Enter a meaningful description to reflect the type of counting journal being created.

E.g., Annual Inventory Count - Sept. 2022.

Site

Select the relevant site from the drop-down selection list.

Warehouse

Select the relevant warehouse from the drop-down selection list.

Warehouse button

Toggle selector button to Yes.

Location button

Toggle selector button to Yes.

Project ID button

Toggle selector button to Yes.
  1. Click OK to proceed.

  1. On the menu at the top of page, click Create Lines and select On-hand from the drop-down selection list.

  1. In the Create on-hand counting journal form, scrol down and expand the Records to include tab and click Filter.

  1. On the Item counting page, select the appropriate query and then click OK.
  2. Click OK again to close the Create on-hand counting journal form.

  1. Review the results. Note that the journal lines display all items and associated on-hand count for items received into the warehouses.

Note: The journal lines display all items and their associated on-hand counts in the selected warehouse/location.

  1. Click X to close the inventory counting journal and return to the list of journals.

  1. Validate that the newly created journal is displayed in the list of journals.

Result

You have created an inventory counting journal for an annual count.

The counting journal can now be printed in preparation to perform the actual count.

Print Counting Journal

Purpose

Use this procedure to generate a counting list for the purpose of performing an inventory count. This could be required for an annual count, periodic count, or end-of-grant count.

Prerequisites

The inventory counting journal has been created in Integra.

Steps

  1. From the Integra home page, navigate the modules to Inventory management à Journal entries à Item counting à Counting.

  1. In the list, select the inventory journal by clicking the journal number.

  1. Click Print > Counting list.

  1. Click OK to proceed.

  1. Review the results of the counting list.
  2. Click the print icon to print the counting list.
    Optional: To export the list, click Export then select the preferred option, e.g., PDF, Excel, etc.
  3. Click X to close the Counting list preview.
  4. Click X to close the Inventory Counting Journal.

Result

You have printed the counting journal.

  • Print multiple copies, as required.
  • Warehouse personnel perform the inventory count in the warehouse using the printed Counting Journal.
  • Upon completion, counting journal is submitted to the Supervisor.

Perform Physical Inventory Count

Purpose

Use this procedure to perform the physical count of inventory.

Prerequisites

The counting list has been generated and printed.

Steps

  1. Staff retrieves hardcopy of counting list.
  2. Staff physically counts all the items for each line number and records the count on the counting list in the Now Counted column.
  3. Staff send or submit the completed counting list to the in-office supply chain staff or warehouse manager.


‎Result

You have performed the physical inventory count and recorded results on paper counting list. The inventory count results can now be entered in Integra.

Record Counting Results

Purpose

Use this procedure to record the physical count in Integra and compare results with recorded on-hand inventory.

Prerequisites

  • Physical count has been completed and results recorded on counting sheet.

 

Steps

  1. From the Integra Home page, navigate the modules to Inventory management > Journal entries > Item counting > Counting.

  1. In the list, open the relevant inventory journal by clicking the journal number.

  1. Click Edit on the menu at the top of page.
     

  1. Under the Counted column, enter the number physically counted for each item as recorded on the counting journal by the warehouse personnel.

    Note:
  • If the number is less than the expected on-hand, the Quantity column displays a negative value. If the counted quantity is greater than the on-hand quantity, the Quantity field displays a positive value

 

  1. Click Save on the menu at the top of page..

Result

You have recorded the counting results in Integra.

  • The counting results can now be posted.

‎

Post Counting Results

Purpose

Use this procedure to post the counting results.

Prerequisites

  • Physical count of items has been recorded in Integra.
  • If counted quantity for an item was less than system quantity (negative discrepancy), the difference in quantity for that item must be “unreserved” from the project before posting the counting journal. Refer to the following procedure for guidance: Unreserve Quantity of Product from a Project

  1. From the Integra Home page, navigate the modules to Inventory management > Journal entries > Item counting > Counting.

  1. In the list, select the relevant inventory journal by clicking the journal number.

  1. Click Post on the menu at the top of the page.

  1. Click OK to proceed.

  1. Validate that an info message displays indicating that the counting journal posted successfully.

Result

You have posted the counting results.

Determining the 50% Lines to Count

Once the lines are created in the counting journal using the counting group 50, which includes all FIFO and FEFO items, the supervisor or manager then decides which items in the list to count. The manager then manually deletes the items not being counted and therefore not being included on the counting journal to be printed for the physical count.

  1. To display the total number of lines in the journal, click the vertical ellipsis icon in the upper right-hand corner of the Journal lines table and select Show footer. The footer at the bottom of the the grid displays the total number of rows.

  1. View the total number of rows displayed in the footer. Then manually determine the lines to be deleted.

  1. Click the checkbox next to the lines that are not being included in the 20% count.
  2. Click Delete to delete the lines from the counting journal.

    Important    : Be sure to use the Delete button for the Journal lines table to delete the counting lines and not the Delete button on the menu bar at the top of the page as this deletes the entire journal.
  3. Click Yes when prompted to delete all marked records.
  4. Click Save on the menu at the top of the page.
  5. Click X to close the inventory counting journal and return to the list of journals.

Inventory adjustment journals are used to "adjust out' the disposal of inventory including expired, damaged, and stolen items negative adjustment).

An adjustment is also used to "adjust in" inventory that is found but is not currently part of the on-hand inventory.(positive adjustment)

Note: This type of journal is not used for cycle count discrepancies.

Post Inventory Adjustment Journal

Purpose

Use this procedure to post an inventory adjustment journal.

Prerequisites

Adjustment journal has been created.

If inventory was "adjusted out" (negative adjustment), the negative quantity of product must be "unreserved" from the project before posting the adjustment journal. Refer to the following procedure for guidance:

  • Unreserve Quantity of Product from a Project

Steps

  1. From the Integra Home page, navigate the modules to Inventory management > Journal entries > Item > Inventory adjustment.

  1. In the list, select the adjustment journal to be posted.

  1. Review the journal line and the Line details section.
  2. Click Post on the menu at the top of the page.

  1. On the Post journal form, click OK to proceed.

  1. Validate that an info-message displays indicating that the journal has been posted.

Result

You have posted an inventory adjustment jou

Follow-on Tasks

 

If inventory was “adjusted in” (positive adjustment), an item forecast line must be created and “marking” must be performed for the subsequent item requirement line in order to be able to successfully pick and pack items for future distribution. Refer to the following procedures in the Item Forecasting to Purchase Order Creation user guide:

  • Create an Item Forecast Line Directly in Integra
  • Perform Marking for an Item Requirement Line

For FEFO items – after the item requirement line is auto-created from the item forecast line, enter the Batch number under the Product dimensions tab of the item requirement line.
