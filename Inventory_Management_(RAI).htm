Business Process Information

Key Components of Inventory Management

The diagram below highlights the key areas that compose Inventory Management in Integra

IRC uses both counting journals and inventory adjustments to make the proper adjustments to on-hand inventory. All inventory journals are posted by an authorized staff; in some instances this can be an in-office supply chain or warehouse manager.

Physical Inventory Counts

IRC has three different scheduled times where cycle counts will be performed. These scenarios apply to CIK inventory as well.

Periodic inventory count (20%count): 20% of products existing are randomly chosen for inventory counting every two months. Products chosen will not repeat within the same year. Last count may be more or less 20%, it will be “count everything that has not yet been counted.”

Annual inventory count (100% count): A full inventory count is performed at the end of the year and warehouses are locked from 1-3 days depending on the country

End of grant count (100% count): Two months before a grant ends, all remaining products procured for a grant that are counted.

Note: A counting journal is also used to account for theft or loss of inventory.

Inventory Adjustments

Inventory adjustments are made upon discovery of discrepancies; not just when the annual cycle count is completed. Adjustments may be required for the following scenarios:

  • Items are expired or damaged
  • It is discovered, while picking, that inventory is missing

If an inventory adjustment is substantial, there is the potential for an insurance claim to be made to help recoup the cost of the inventory loss/adjustment

Supply Chain posts adjustments to a holding account

Finance, in coordination with in-office Supply Chain or equivalent, executive director, etc., account for any adjustments in inventory that will have a financial impact to the organization (i.e., spoilage uncovered during cycle count.)

Process Flow Diagram

  • Upon receipt of products, staff needs to manage the on-hand inventory.
  • All tasks are managed in Integra except for the physical inventory count.
  • The physical count may be a segregated function from the recording and posting of the count results.

Create Counting Journal

Purpose

Use this procedure to create a new counting journal for an annual inventory count in Integra.

Prerequisites

Not applicable.

Steps

  1. From the Integra home page, navigate modules to Inventory management >Journal entries >Item counting >Counting.

  1. Click New on the toolbar at the top of the page.

  1. Complete/select the following fields/buttons:

Field / Button

Entry/Selection Required

Site

Select the site from the drop-down selection list.

Warehouse

Select the warehouse from the drop-down selection list.

Warehouse button

Click button to change to Yes.

Location button

Click button to change to Yes.

Project ID button

Click button to change to Yes.
  1. Click OK to proceed.

  1. On the toolbar at the top of page, click Create lines >On-hand.
  2. In the Create on-hand counting journal form, scroll down and expand the Records to include tab and click Filter.

  1. In the Items counting section, select the appropriate query then click OK.
  2. Review the results. Note that the journal lines display all items and associated on-hand count received in the warehouses.
  3. Click X to close the page.

    The inventory counting journal is displayed below.

Result

You have created an inventory counting journal.

The counting journal can now be printed in preparation to perform the actual count.

Print Counting Journal

Purpose

Use this procedure to generate a counting list for the purpose of performing an inventory count. This could be required for an annual count, periodic count, or end-of-grant count

Prerequisites

  • The inventory counting journal has been created in Integra

Steps

  1. From the Integra home page, navigate the modules to Inventory management >Journal entries >Item counting >Counting.

  1. In the list, select the desired inventory journal by clicking the journal number.

  1. Click Print then select Counting list.

  1. In the Counting list, click OK.

  1. Review the results of the counting list.
  1. To export the list, click Export then select the preferred option, e.g., PDF, Excel, etc.

  1. Print the counting list.

Result

You have printed the counting list.

  • The physical inventory count can now be performed.

Perform Physical Inventory Count

Purpose

Use this procedure to perform the physical count of inventory.

Prerequisites

  • The counting list has been generated and printed.

Steps

  1. Staff retrieves hardcopy of counting list.
  2. Staff physically counts all the items for each line number and records the count on the counting list.
  3. Staff hands out or sends the completed counting list to the in-office supply chain staff or warehouse manager.

‎

Result

You have performed the physical inventory count and recorded results on paper counting list.

  • The inventory count results can now be entered in Integra.

Record Counting Results

Purpose

Use this procedure to record the physical count in Integra and compare results with recorded on-hand inventory.

Prerequisites

  • Physical count has been completed and results recorded on counting sheet.

‎

Steps

  1. From the Integra home page, navigate the modules to Inventory management >Journal entries >Item counting >Counting.

  1. In the list, select the desired inventory journal by clicking the journal number.

  1. Click Edit on the toolbar at the top of page.

  1. Under the Counted column, enter the number physically counted for each item.
    Note: If the number is less than the expected on-hand, the Quantity column displays a negative value. If the counted quantity is greater than the on-hand quantity, the quantity field displays a positive value.
  2. Click Save on the toolbar at the top of page.
  3. Optional: To export the list, click Export then select the preferred option, e.g., PDF, Excel, etc.

Result

You have recorded the counting results in Integra.

The counting results can now be posted.

Post Counting Results

Purpose

Use this procedure to post the counting results.

Prerequisites

  • Physical count of items has been recorded in Integra.

Steps

  1. From the Integra home page, navigate the modules to Inventory management >Journal entries >Item counting >Counting.

  1. In the list, select the desired inventory journal by clicking the journal number.

  1. Click Post on the toolbar at the top of the page.

  1. Click OK.

  1. Validate that a posting success message is displayed at top of form.

Display On-hand Inventory

Purpose

Use this procedure to verify on-hand inventory associated with each stocked product received in inventory.

Prerequisites

The item has been set up as an inventory item in Integra.

Steps

  1. From the Integra home page, navigate the modules to Inventory management >Inquiries and reports >On-Hand list.
  2. Apply any or all of the following filters by selecting the drop-down list for more filtering options:
    • Item number
    • Site
    • Warehouse

      Note: If additional filters are needed, select Add filter fields to select other values such as Project ID or Location.
  3. Click Dimensions on the menu at the top of the page to add additional inventory dimension values to the on-hand inquiry table.

  1. As required, select the checkboxes next to the relevant selections, e.g., Site, Warehouse, Location, Project ID, and Item number.
  2. Click OK to proceed.

  1. Click Apply on the left-hand Filters pane to retrieve results.
  2. Review the results of on-hand inventory.

Result

You have viewed the on-hand quantity for an inventory item.

Create Inventory Adjustment Journal

Purpose

Use this procedure to create an adjustment journal to record inventory discrepancies.

Inventory adjustment journals should be used to record the disposal of inventory including expired, damaged, and stolen items.

An adjustment is also used when inventory is found but is not currently part of the on-hand inventory.

Prerequisites

  • It has been determined that an inventory adjustment is required.

Steps

  1. From the Integra home page, navigate the modules to Inventory management >Journal entries >Item >Inventory adjustment.

  1. Click New on the menu at the top of the page.

  1. Complete the following fields:
    • Name: Leave default of INV_ADJ.
    • Enter Site and Warehouse
  2. Click OK to proceed.

  1. Under Journal lines section, click New.

  1. In the Item number field, enter the relevant item number.
  2. In the Quantity field, enter the adjusted quantity.
  3. Scroll down to the Line details section.

  1. On the Inventory dimensions tab and complete the following fields:
    • Project ID
    • Location
  2. Click the Financial dimension tab.
  3. Populate the D1 through D6 financial dimensions. Contact your Finance team for guidance on selecting the correct dimensions.
  4. Click Save on the menu at the top of the page.

Result

You have created an inventory adjustment journal.

The adjustment journal can now be posted.

Post Inventory Adjustment Journal

Receive CIK Inventory Items

Purpose

Use this procedure to record the receipt of CIK items into inventory. This only applies to CIK items that are tracked in inventory, e.g., large and/or high value items.

Note: Receipts are not entered for non-inventory CIK items in RAI offices as they are only tracked at the point of distribution.

Prerequisites

  • CIK item has been set up as an inventory item in Integra.

Steps

  1. From the Integra home page, navigate the modules Inventory management >Journal entries >Items >Movement.

  1. On the menu at the top, click New.

  1. In the Name field, select CIK_In_Mov from the drop-down selection list.
    Note: When CIK_INV_MO is selected, the Offset account field is automatically populated with account 41600.
  2. Click OK to proceed.

  1. Under the Journal lines section, click New to add a new journal line.

  1. Complete the following fields for the journal line:

Field

Entry/Selection Required

Date

Value defaults to today’s date.

Item number

Enter the CIK item number or click the drop-down arrow to perform a search for the item.

Note: Ensure the item description starts with CIK.

Product name

This value defaults after the item number is entered.

Note: CIK items begin with “CIK” in the product description.

Quantity

Enter the quantity being received.

Cost price

Enter the price of the CIK item being received.

Additional Notes:

Cost price should be entered based on the price given by the donor.

If the donor did not give a price, IRC will need to calculate the fair market value. Market value will be calculated following IRC’s internal policies.

Cost price is always in the legal entity accounting currency which is USD for all entities. Market value will be calculated following IRC’s internal policies.

If the fair market value is given to IRC in a different currency, this needs to be a manual calculation to convert to USD.

Cost amount

This value defaults based on the auto-calculation of cost price X quantity.
  1. Scroll down to the Line details section and click the Financial dimension tab.

  1. Enter/validate the following financial dimension fields.

Field

Entry/Selection Required

D01Project_Code

If items received are for a specific project, enter the specific D01 project ID. If not, enter CK001.

D04Location

Enter the relevant D4 code.

E.g., UGAAT

D05Entity_Code

This value defaults based on legal entity.

D11Revenue_Stream

Select OMSC - Miscellaneous.

D12Fundraising_Credit

Select the relevant D12 code based on location.

Note: The D9 (client ID) code is not required when receiving CIK inventory; it is only entered at point of distribution.

  1. Click the Inventory dimensions tab.

  1. Complete the following fields:

Field

Entry/Selection Required

Project ID

Enter the project ID.

This must match the value entered in the D1 field on the Financial dimensions tab.

Site

This value defaults.

Warehouse

Select the relevant warehouse from the drop-down selection list.

Location

Select the relevant location from the drop-down selection list.
  1. Double-check that the Cost price field in the Journal lines section reflects the price previously entered.
  2. On the menu at the top, click Save to check for any errors.

  1. On the menu at the top, click Post.

  1. Click OK to proceed.

  1. Validate that the posting success message appears. (It appears in a light blue bar near the top of the page.)

Result

You have recorded the receipt of CIK inventory items in Integra.

  • To determine the current on-hand quantity for an item, refer to the procedure in this guide titled, Display On-hand Inventory.

Note: You may see a list of previously created item requirements in the Lines table.

  1. On the menu at top of page, click New

Project ID

Product name

Item number

Note: Ensure the item description starts with CIK, e.g., CIK Mattress - Full

Quantity

Enter the quantity being distributed.

Important: This value defaults to “1”. Ensure it is updated to reflect the actual quantity being distributed.

Line property

This value defaults to NonBill from the project setup. Leave this default value.

Category

E.g., 75000.0.i Program Supplies & Material

Note: You may need to scroll to the right to see this field.

Field / Button

  1. Click Lines to return to the line item view.

  1. On the menu at top of page, click Manage.

  1. On the action pane, click Inventory and select Marking from the drop-down selection list.


Note: It is possible that the same type of item was donated with different fair market values. To ensure the correct cost price is posted, “marking” is used before the packing slip is posted. Marking allows you to override FIFO costing.

  1. Review the lines to identify the record with the correct cost price.

    Note:
  • Lines display for all on-hand inventory for that site, warehouse and location.
  • Each line represents a different transaction (movement journal) that the inventory was received through.

  1. On the menu at top of page, click Manage.

Attach Signed Packing List to Vendor Record

Purpose

Use this procedure to attach the client-signed packing list to the client vendor record.

Prerequisites

  • Packing list has been printed.
  • Client has signed the hard copy of the packing list.

Steps

  1. From the Integra home page, navigate the modules Accounts Payable >Vendors >All vendors.
  2. Search for and select the client vendor record.

  1. In the top right corner of page, click the paper clip icon.

  1. On the menu at the top of page, click New and select File from the drop-down selection list.

  1. In the right-hand panel, click Browse.
  2. Locate and select relevant document, as per standard Windows browsing functionality. (It may take a few seconds for upload to complete)

  1. Validate the attachment is listed in the left-hand panel.
  2. Click X in the right-hand corner of page to close the window and return to the previous screen.

Result

You have uploaded (attached) a signed packing list to a client’s vendor record.
