Business Process Overview

Business Process Flow

Purchase Requisition to Payment  

Three-way Matching

The three-way match compares the pricing and quantities in the PO, the product receipt and the invoice. If the quantities and prices match, the three-way match is considered successful and the vendor can be paid. 

If there are discrepancies in the matching process, AP (Accounts Payable) must investigate and resolve the differences before payment to the vendor can be made.  

PO Invoice Processing

The use of purchase orders allow approvers, Accounts Payable, and Supply Chain, to easilyand quickly track vendor and purchase-related documentation.  

When a vendor invoice is received by IRC Finance, the received PO can be invoiced.   

Non-PO Invoice Processing

IRC processes many invoices that do not originate with a PR/PO. These are often payments made to beneficiaries as direct assistance, smaller procurement of offices supplies or perishable goods, purchases of assets by refugees through a third party, recurring bills, sub-grants, or contracts below $2500.

Non-PO invoices may also be used for FCM travel, Box, phone bills, etc.

Process for automatic EFT Processing

  1. You must submit payment journal to workflow 
  2. When the workflow is approved, a payment file in TAS will be created and processed automatically.  
  3. Once all the payments submitted are accepted by the bank, the payment journal will automatically post. You will not have to follow the Manual Posting Process. 
  4. For the finance user, sending the payment out to the bank and submitting it to the workflow is the final step.  
  5. If there was an error or a rejection in one of the payments, then the journal will not automatically be posted. 
  6. If one payment isn’t in the journal, then fix it and resubmit to workflow. 
  7. If 9 out of 10 payments were accepted, remove the rejected payment and post the correct 9 payments. 
  8. Correct the pending payment and resubmit to workflow.  
  9. Once approved, it will automatically go to the bank, and if accepted, the journal will auto-post.

Processing PO Prepayments

In order to enable advance payment to a vendor, a posting profile must be set up for that vendor group. This is a one-time setup per vendor group.

For guidance of applying posting profiles, refer to the section titled, Posting Profiles for Prepayment Setup. 

End-to-end Business Process

1. Supply Chain identifies the vendor request for a prepayment(s) on purchase order during the quotation collection process.\

2. Supply Chain will share, via email, the prepayment(s) request details and get a repy with the approval of the authorized person approving the PO in Integra. The email and the approval reply are considered auditible supporting documents and must be attached to the PO.

  • E-mail must include PO number, vendor name, vendor number, prepayment value(s) and requested date(s) of the prepayment(s)

3. Supply Chain records prepayment request details in the Prepayment form under the Purchase action tab on the purchase order.

4. If more than one prepayment is requested, Supply Chain shall adhere to the following guidance in populating the prepayment form:

  • Description: E.g., “2 Prepayments: 1st=50%, 2nd=50%”, etc.
  • Value: Value must be equal to the total value of the prepayments in percentage or money amount

5. Supply Chain informs Finance of the vendor’s prepayment request and attaches the approval email to the PO page in Integra. Finance receives prepayment invoice from vendor.

6. Finance processes payment for vendor using the advance vendor payment journal, attaching the vendor invoice as a supporting document, and reviewing the vendor transactions to avoid duplication.

Important: The purchase order number must be entered in the Description field on the advance vendor payment journal to create a link to the correct PO during liquidation, and to allow the reviewer to know which PO to review. In the case of multiple prepayments, the Description must also reflect the sequence of the prepayment. For example, the first payment description would be PO00000452/1st/50% and the second payment would be PO00000452/2nd/30%.

7. Finance informs Supply Chain that the prepayment is processed detailing out the amount and PO number.

8. Once the final invoice is received, Finance liquidates (settles) the amount for the advance vendor payment journal (or journals) related to the PO using the settlement function on the PO Invoicing tab.

9. Finance processes the final vendor invoice for the outstanding amount on the purchase order, per standard PO invoicing process, if any.

10. Finance processes vendor payment journal for outstanding amount after reconciling the vendor account, if applicable.

11. Finance and Supply Chain must collaborate to reconcile prepayments at least monthly, in addition to when final invoices are received.

Prepayment Scenarios

  1. Vendor requires single prepayment:
    1. Supply Chain should
      1. Enter all known information on the Prepayment form in the purchase order.
      2. Communicate prepayment details to Finance/Accounts Payable (AP) via e-mail. Supply Chain must attach the email/form approval by authorized personnel to the PO.
    2. Supply Chain and Finance/AP must follow the steps outlined in the end-to-end business process above and in the system steps below. Guidance can also be found in the relevant training user guides.

  2. Vendor requires multiple prepayments:
    1. Supply Chain must
      1. Enter all known information the Prepayment form in the purchase order and
      2. Communicate prepayment details to Finance/Accounts Payable (AP) via e-mail.
    2. The PO Prepayment form cannot accommodate information for multiple prepayments. Supply Chain must therefore include all information in the Description field, and the prepayment(s) total in the Value field following the example mentioned in step 3 outlined in the step-by-step system procedure below.
    3. Finance/AP will process each prepayment using the advance vendor payment journal ensuring that the PO number is added in the Description field for each transaction until the final purchase order invoice is received.

  3. Prepayment is 100% of the purchase order amount:
    1. Supply Chain and Finance should follow the steps outlined in the end-to-end process above.
    2. After the prepayment is paid to the vendor by Finance, Finance should expect an invoice from the vendor once the procurement is fulfilled.
    3. Upon liquidation, Finance will make the 100% advance payment through the open transactions on the PO invoice.
      1. Upon settling this transaction, the remaining invoice amount will be reduced to 0% (nothing left to be paid to the vendor) and the project will be charged.

Important Considerations

How do we avoid overpaying a vendor?

Before proceeding with any payment journals (prepayment or final invoice), Finance/AP should review the purchase order and all open transactions on the vendor record. If still unclear, consult Supply Chain on any known payments made to the vendor.

The PO number should be included by the vendor on any payment request and/or invoice.

  1. Access transactions on the vendor record by going to Modules: Accounts Payable: All Vendors: Select Vendor: Click the vendor tab and select all transactions. Search for description with PO# description.
  2. Alternatively, you can access the related vendor records directly from the PO by hovering over the vendor account number and clicking the hyperlink on the vendor ID number.

How can Supply Chain check whether a prepayment has been paid to the vendor?

  1. The user can use PowerBI to access payment details for TRADE/CASH vendors, or
  2. Check with Finance/AP.

How can finance avoid duplicate prepayments due to the payment not linked directly to the PO?

  1. Review vendor transactions to ensure that there are no duplicates.
  2. Finance must ensure that the invoice number on the vendor payment journal is entered and is an exact match of the vendor invoice number to avoid duplication.
  3. Review the following reports in Integra to reconcile vendor account: Modules à Accounts Payable Module à Inquiries and Reports
    1. Vendor Aging Report.
    2. Vendor Open Transactions Report
    3. Vendor transactions report

How can finance and SC avoid inaccurate prepayments amounts?

  1. SC must review the prepayment request by the vendor and reflect it on the prepayment tab on the PO accurately.
  2. Finance must review the approval email attached to the PO and match it with the prepayment details on the PO.

Managing Bank Returns

Bank returns happens when someone processes a payment from Integra and sends the payment to the bank. The bank has certain banking criteria that must be met before the payment is processed. For instance, the name of the payee, account number, routing number, etc. If any of this payment information is incorrect, the bank will reject the payment. When the bank rejects the payment, the bank might charge a fee for the return of funds, however, sometimes there are no fees.  Therefore, we are going to look at how to book bank return entries with and without fees when EFT is the payment method. 

Bank Returns Without Fees

  1. Invoice – Received from vendor 
  2. Payment – Send payment to the Bank by EFT 
  3. Return Payment Reversal – Use the EFT “reverse posted payment”. 
  4. Unsettle the payment against invoice 1 and 2 and settle 2 and 3. 
  5. New payment journal – Resend payment to the Bank.
  6. Settle 1 and 5 (This will automatically settle when you use the settlement functionality in the vendor payment journal). 

‎

Bank Returns With Fees

Here is a quick summary of steps that need to happen in order to properly book returns in Integra when the EFT payment method is used. 

  1. Invoice – Received from vendor 
  2. Payment – Send payment to the Bank by EFT  
  3. Return Payment Reversal and Bank fee – Use General Journal to book the reversal.
  4. Unsettle the payment against invoice 1 and 2. AND Settle 2 and 3. 
  5. New payment journal – Resend payment to the Bank. 
  6. Settle 1 and 5 (This will automatically settle when you use the settlement functionality in the vendor payment journal). 

‎

Note: The only step that is different from booking returns without fee mentioned above to booking returns with bank fees is STEP 3.  For Bank Returns with Fee, we are going to use the General Journals to book the return.  This is because we need to use Project for the bank fee. Note that you cannot use the Project sub-ledger in the Vendor Payment Journal.

VAT and Withholding Tax

Some countries in which the IRC operates are not exempt from VAT (value added tax), such as Sierra Leone and Uganda*. In these situations, the applicable VAT rate must be applied on all affected vendor invoices entered into Integra.

*There may be a few projects that are granted VAT exemption, e.g., World Bank projects and USAID projects. In these instances, the VAT charge is reimbursed.

Alternatively, some vendors may be subject to a withholding tax. In these situations, the applicable withholding tax rate must be applied on all vendor payment journals for the vendor.

In some cases, a vendor may be subject to both VAT and a withholding tax so both must be applied respectively.

Process Intercompany Charge from Expense Management

Purpose

Finance uses this procedure to process a payment for an intercompany charge that originated from a posted expense report.

These steps should be part of Finance’s daily or weekly activities.

Example:

A.) Staff in Company A (e.g., 6DAT) submit expense reports with Intercompany charges to Company B (e.g., 9IRC).

  • Company A supervisor reviews and approves expense report
  • Company A Finance reviews and posts expense report

B.) Company A Finance creates an Intercompany customer invoice to push the intercompany charges to Company B.

C.) Company B Finance searches for the Pending vendor invoice for the intercompany charge from Company A and sends it for approval and then posts the invoice.

Prerequisites

  • The expense report with the intercompany charges has been approved  and posted. 

Steps

Create New Intercompany Invoice from Originating Legal Entity

  1. From the Integra home page, navigate modulees to Project management and accounting > Project invoices > Intercompany customer invoice.
  2. Click New to create a new intercompany invoice. 
  3. Toggle the indicator to Yes to for Include all Legal Entities.
  4. Click Search to display a list of all outstanding intercompany transactions. 
  5. Click the checkbox under the Selected column to select the relevant intercompany transaction for which to process payment. 
  6. Click OK to proceed. 

The Intercompany customer invoice is generated from the journal.

  1. Click Post in the Functions action pane.

  1. Click OK to complete the posting. 

Company B Finance searches for the pending vendor invoice for the intercompany charge from Company A.

  1. Navigate modules to Accounts payable > Pending vendor invoices.

  1. On the Number column of the pending vendor invoices grid, search for all invoice numbers that begin with "FT" (free text) and select the invoice to process. 

  1. Review the pending vendor invoice. (The vendor is Company A.) 
Note: If the project /D1 is closed, this is the opportunity to recode to a different project.
  1. Submit the invoice to Workflow for approval. 
  2. Post the invoice after approval is complete. 
  3. Process vendor payment via vendor payment journal. 

Result

  • You have processed an intercompany charge that originated from a posted expense report.
