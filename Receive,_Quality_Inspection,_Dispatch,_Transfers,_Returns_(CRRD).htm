Receipt and Quality Inspection - Business Process Information

Business Process Flow Diagram

Documents Processed in Receiving Process

The procedures below specify the sequence for first in first out (FIFO) and first expiry first out (FEFO) items in CRRD:

  1. Receipts list – Inventory personnel post a Receipts List when the product is received from the supplier. The Receipts List does not have an impact on financials or physical inventory.
  2. Quality order – Once the Receipts List is posted, a Quality Order is automatically generated. A quality inspection is performed and results recorded. The quality inspection is performed by technical personnel from the requesting department.
  3. Product Registration – All stock items (FIFO and FEFO) need to be registered to the correct location by Supply Chain personnel, i.e., change from Receiving location to actual location items will be stored. Pharmacy items are assigned a vendor batch number and food items are assigned a system batch number.
  4. Product receipt – A Product Receipt is recorded and posted. This is a prerequisite to invoice processing and three-way match. The posting of the product receipt updates physical inventory (for stock items) and financials.

Important: When products, that were initially received/posted at a port of entry, arrive at the IRC warehouse, a Transfer Journal must be processed to record the transfer to the actual storage location inside IRC warehouse where the product has been physically stored.  

General Information for Receiving Stock Items

All stocked items go through inspection and receipt upon arrival at an IRC location. They will be quality controlled for all CRRD legal entities and some RAI entities. When material arrives or services are delivered at an IRC location, Integra must be updated to record the receipt of the material.  The receipt record is a prerequisite to invoice payment.

Stock products are received and tracked in inventory. i.e., on-hand balances are updated with each product receipt.

A partial quantity of a PO may be received. In these situations, the PO remains open until all items are received.

Important: Services are received the same way non-stock items are received.

Product Dispatch - Business Process Flow 

Product Dispatch - General Information

The IRC uses the Integra pick/pack function for the following tasks:

  • Distributing material from the IRC warehouse to a beneficiary
  • Transferring material from an IRC warehouse/dispensary to another IRC warehouse/dispensary

The pick/pack function is triggered by either a transfer order or project consumption of items. Each of these actions automatically generate picking instructions for Supply Chain to review. Warehouse personnel print the picking list for the items to be shipped.

The picking list is used to go through the warehouse for picking the item quantities. Packing lists for item requirements are posted in order to distribute inventory. When a packing list is posted, inventory is reduced from both financial and physical inventory. The financial inventory is the inventory main account cost balance and physical inventory is the on-hand list quantity. The project expense is also posted.

IMPORTANT: Communication between Programs and Supply Chain is key to ensuring the correct items are being dispatched at the right time, in the right quantity.

E.g., The Programs person creating the picking list can print/share the picking list and obtain an approval signature (or email authorization) from the authorized Programs personnel. This information should then be provided to Supply Chain personnel who perform the physical picking of items and post the packing slip.

Update Purchase Order Quantity via Request Change Function

Purpose

Use this procedure to change a purchase order after it has been approved.

Example:

A quantity of purchase order shipment from the vendor did not not pass quality inspection and the vendor cannot provide replacement items. In this case, the quantity on PO must be reduced to reflect only the quantity of items that passed inspection.

Prerequisites

Purchase order has been approved and/or confirmed.

Steps

  1. Navigate modules to Procurement and sourcing > Purchase orders > All purchase orders.
  2. Select and open the relevant purchase order.
  3. Click the Purchase order tab on the top menu.

  1. Under the Maintain section, click Request change  to make a change to the order.

Note: Once you click Request change on an order that has a status of Confirmed, the status returns to Draft.

  1. In the Quantity field, update the quantity to the number of items that passed inspection.

Note: Validate/update the price on the line as sometimes it disappears after changing the quantity.

  1. Click Save  to save the update.
  2. Click Workflow > Submit to resubmit the purchase order for approval.
Note: After the purchase order is approved, it requires confirmation again. This step is performed by Supply Chain. Once this step is complete, the purchase order status will return to Open Order and the approval status will return to Confirmed.

Result

You have changed a purchase order using the "Request Change" function. an invoice can now be processed for the purchase order.

Processing Items that Fail Quality Inspection - Scenarios

Scenario Steps
IRC refuses to receive defective quantity and supplier ACCEPTS to send full replacement of defective quantity

Prerequisite: Quantity of failed items have been recorded in a quality order; failed items have been blocked from inventory; a Non conformance has been created, approved, and closed.

  1. Supply Chain informs supplier about refused quantity.
  2. Once replacement items are sent by supplier complete the steps outlined in the training user guide: Product Receipt and Dispatch.

IRC refuses to receive defective quantity and supplier cannot replace any quantity of the defective items

Prerequisite: Quantity of failed items have been recorded in a quality order; failed items have been blocked from inventory; a Non conformance has been created, approved, and closed.

  1. Supply Chain opens PO and selects Request change option.
  2. Supply Chain updates the PO line quantity with only the quantity of items that passed inspection.
  3. Supply Chain submits PO for approval, confirms it and sends updated PO to vendor.

IRC refuses to receive defective quantity and supplier sends replacement for partial quantity of defective items  

Prerequisite: Quantity of failed items have been recorded in a quality order; failed items have been blocked from inventory; a Non conformance has been created, approved, and closed.

  1. Supply Chain informs supplier about refused quantity.
  2. Supply Chain opens original PO and selects Request change option.
  3. Supply Chain updates the quantity in the PO line by deducting the quantity not being replaced by the supplier.
    1. Ensure the Unit price field reflects the original purchase price; if not, update it.
  4. Supply Chain submits PO for approval, confirms it and sends updated PO to vendor.
  5. Once replacement items are sent by supplier repeat steps 1 through 8 above for the replacement quantity.
Pharmaceutical company recalls product. If invoice for full quantity was already processed, Supply Chain creates a Return Purchase Order to obtain credit from vendor for the quantity of items not accepted.
Receipt for full quantity was posted at port of entry but defects were found when products arrived at IRC warehouse.

If invoice for full quantity was already processed, Supply Chain creates a Return Purchase Order to obtain credit from vendor for the quantity of items not accepted.

If invoice has not been issued yet, vendor should issue invoice for only quantity received and accepted, i.e., the quantity on the Change PO.
Return of non-stock items to vendor

If invoice for full quantity was already processed, Supply Chain creates a Return Purchase Order to obtain credit from vendor for the quantity of items not accepted.

If invoice has not been issued yet, vendor should issue invoice for only quantity received and accepted, i.e., the quantity on the Change PO.
